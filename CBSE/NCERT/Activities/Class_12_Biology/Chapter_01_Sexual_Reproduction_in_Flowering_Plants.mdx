# Class 12 Biology - Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants
## Activities

### Activity 1.1: Flower Observation
**Aim:** To identify and list flowers of ornamental, social, and cultural value.
**Procedure:**
1. Visit a nearby garden or home and identify at least five flowers of ornamental value.
2. Discuss with your family members and find out the names of five flowers used in social and cultural celebrations.
3. Research the term 'floriculture' and its significance.
**Observation:** You will find a wide variety of flowers like Roses, Hibiscus, Marigold, Jasmine, etc., each with unique aesthetic and cultural roles.

### Activity 1.2: Variation in Stamens
**Aim:** To appreciate the large variation in size and shape of stamens in different flowers.
**Procedure:**
1. Collect a stamen each from ten flowers of different species.
2. Arrange them on a microscope slide.
3. Observe each stamen under a dissecting microscope.
4. Draw neat diagrams to show the range in shape and attachment of anthers.
**Observation:** Stamens vary significantly in length, filament thickness, and anther shape across different species.

### Activity 1.3: Observation of Pollen Grains
**Aim:** To study the architecture of pollen grains from different species.
**Procedure:**
1. Collect opened anthers of Hibiscus or any other flower.
2. Touch the anthers to find yellowish powdery pollen grains.
3. Sprinkle these grains on a drop of water on a glass slide.
4. Observe under a microscope.
**Observation:** You will see a variety of sizes, shapes, colours, and surface designs (exine patterns) on the pollen grains.

### Activity 1.4: Pollen Germination
**Aim:** To study pollen germination and pollen tube growth.
**Procedure:**
1. Prepare a 10% sucrose solution (optionally with boric acid, calcium nitrate, magnesium sulfate, and potassium nitrate).
2. Place a drop of this solution on a slide and sprinkle some pollen grains (e.g., from Pea or Balsam).
3. Wait for 15-30 minutes.
4. Observe under the microscope.
**Observation:** Pollen tubes will emerge from the germ pores of the pollen grains.

### Activity 1.5: Observation of Ovules and Pistils
**Aim:** To study the parts of the gynoecium.
**Procedure:**
1. Dissect a flower (e.g., Hibiscus) to isolate the pistil.
2. Identify the stigma, style, and ovary.
3. Carefully cut the ovary to observe the ovarian cavity (locule) and placenta.
4. Observe different types of pistils (monocarpellary, multicarpellary, syncarpous, apocarpous) in various flowers.
**Observation:** Different flowers show variations in the fusion and number of carpels.

### Activity 1.6: Seed Dissection
**Aim:** To observe the various parts of the embryo and the seed.
**Procedure:**
1. Soak a few seeds of wheat, maize, peas, chickpeas, and groundnut in water overnight.
2. Split the seeds carefully.
3. Identify the seed coat, cotyledons, and embryonal axis.
**Observation:** Dicot seeds have two cotyledons, while monocot seeds have one (scutellum).

### Activity 1.7: Polyembryony in Citrus
**Aim:** To observe multiple embryos in a single seed.
**Procedure:**
1. Take a few seeds of an orange.
2. Squeeze the seeds and observe the contents under a magnifying lens.
**Observation:** You may see several embryos of different sizes and shapes protruding from a single seed.
