# Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

## Key Concepts

### Flower – A Fascination Organ
Flowers are not just aesthetic objects; they are the sites of sexual reproduction in angiosperms. A typical flower has four whorls: calyx, corolla, androecium (male), and gynoecium (female).

### Pre-fertilisation: Structures and Events
Several hormonal and structural changes lead to the differentiation and development of the floral primordium.

#### 1. Stamen, Microsporangium and Pollen Grain
*   **Stamen:** Consists of a long stalk (filament) and a terminal bilobed structure (anther).
*   **Anther:** Typically bilobed and dithecous. It is a four-sided (tetragonal) structure with four microsporangia.
*   **Microsporogenesis:** The process of formation of microspores from a pollen mother cell (PMC) through meiosis.
*   **Pollen Grain:** Represents the male gametophyte.
    *   **Exine:** Hard outer layer made of **sporopollenin**, the most resistant organic material. It has germ pores.
    *   **Intine:** Inner thin wall made of cellulose and pectin.
    *   **Cells:** Mature pollen has a **vegetative cell** (large, food reserve) and a **generative cell** (small, spindle-shaped, gives rise to two male gametes).

#### 2. The Pistil, Megasporangium (ovule) and Embryo sac
*   **Gynoecium:** Can be monocarpellary or multicarpellary (syncarpous or apocarpous).
*   **Ovule:** Attached to the placenta by a funicle. Parts include hilum, integuments, micropyle, chalaza, and nucellus.
*   **Megasporogenesis:** Formation of megaspores from the megaspore mother cell (MMC) via meiosis.
*   **Embryo Sac (Female Gametophyte):** Formed from a single functional megaspore (**monosporic development**). A mature embryo sac is **8-nucleate and 7-celled**.
    *   **Egg Apparatus:** 2 synergids + 1 egg cell.
    *   **Antipodals:** 3 cells at the chalazal end.
    *   **Central Cell:** Contains 2 polar nuclei.

### Pollination
The transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma.
*   **Autogamy:** Within the same flower.
*   **Geitonogamy:** Different flower of the same plant.
*   **Xenogamy:** Different flower of a different plant (Genetically different).
*   **Agents:** Abiotic (wind, water) and Biotic (bees, butterflies, birds, bats, etc.).

### Double Fertilisation
A unique event in angiosperms.
1.  **Syngamy:** One male gamete + Egg cell → Zygote (2n).
2.  **Triple Fusion:** Second male gamete + 2 Polar Nuclei → Primary Endosperm Nucleus (PEN) (3n).

### Post-fertilisation: Structures and Events
*   **Endosperm:** Develops from the primary endosperm cell; provides nutrition to the embryo.
*   **Embryo:** Develops at the micropylar end. Stages: Proembryo → Globular → Heart-shaped → Mature.
*   **Seed:** Fertilised ovule.
    *   **Albuminous:** Retain endosperm (e.g., wheat, maize).
    *   **Non-albuminous:** No residual endosperm (e.g., pea, groundnut).
    *   **Perisperm:** Persistent nucellus (e.g., black pepper).
*   **Fruit:** Ripened ovary.
    *   **True Fruits:** Develop from ovary.
    *   **False Fruits:** Thalamus also contributes (e.g., apple, strawberry).
    *   **Parthenocarpic:** Formed without fertilisation (e.g., banana).

### Apomixis and Polyembryony
*   **Apomixis:** Production of seeds without fertilisation (asexual reproduction mimicking sexual).
*   **Polyembryony:** Occurrence of more than one embryo in a seed (common in Citrus and Mango).
