# Excretory Products and their Elimination

## Key Concepts

### Types of Nitrogenous Wastes
Animals excrete nitrogenous wastes primarily in three forms, depending on their habitat and water availability:
1.  **Ammonia (Ammonotelism):** Highly toxic, requires large amounts of water for elimination. Excreted by many bony fishes, aquatic amphibians, and aquatic insects.
2.  **Urea (Ureotelism):** Less toxic than ammonia, requires less water. Mammals, terrestrial amphibians, and marine fishes convert ammonia to urea in the liver.
3.  **Uric Acid (Uricotelism):** Least toxic, excreted as a pellet or paste with minimum water loss. Common in reptiles, birds, land snails, and insects.

### Human Excretory System
The human excretory system consists of:
*   **A pair of kidneys:** Reddish-brown, bean-shaped structures.
*   **A pair of ureters:** Tubes that carry urine to the bladder.
*   **Urinary bladder:** Stores urine until micturition.
*   **Urethra:** Tube through which urine is released.

### Structure of the Kidney
*   **Cortex:** The outer zone of the kidney.
*   **Medulla:** The inner zone, divided into medullary pyramids.
*   **Nephron:** The functional unit of the kidney, consisting of:
    *   **Glomerulus:** A tuft of capillaries for filtration.
    *   **Renal Tubule:** Includes Bowman's capsule, Proximal Convoluted Tubule (PCT), Henle's loop, and Distal Convoluted Tubule (DCT).

### Urine Formation
Urine formation involves three main processes:
1.  **Glomerular Filtration:** Blood is filtered in the glomerulus; GFR is about 125 ml/min.
2.  **Reabsorption:** Nearly 99% of the filtrate is reabsorbed by the renal tubules (active or passive).
3.  **Secretion:** Tubular cells secrete H+, K+, and ammonia into the filtrate to maintain ionic balance.

### Functions of Renal Tubules
*   **PCT:** Major site for reabsorption of electrolytes, water, and essential nutrients.
*   **Henle’s Loop:** Maintains high osmolarity in the medullary interstitium; helps in concentrating urine.
*   **DCT:** Conditional reabsorption of Na+ and water; maintains pH and sodium-potassium balance.
*   **Collecting Duct:** Reabsorbs large amounts of water to produce concentrated urine.

### Mechanism of Concentration of the Filtrate
The **Counter Current Mechanism** (involving Henle’s loop and vasa recta) creates an osmolarity gradient in the medulla. This allows human kidneys to produce urine that is nearly four times more concentrated than the initial filtrate.

### Regulation of Kidney Function
Regulated by hormonal feedback mechanisms:
*   **ADH (Vasopressin):** Facilitates water reabsorption to prevent diuresis.
*   **Renin-Angiotensin Mechanism:** Triggered by a fall in GFR; increases blood pressure and GFR.
*   **ANF (Atrial Natriuretic Factor):** Opposes the renin-angiotensin mechanism by causing vasodilation when blood flow to the atria increases.

### Disorders of the Excretory System
*   **Uremia:** Accumulation of urea in blood; treated with hemodialysis.
*   **Renal Calculi:** Stones of crystallized salts formed in the kidney.
*   **Glomerulonephritis:** Inflammation of the glomeruli.
