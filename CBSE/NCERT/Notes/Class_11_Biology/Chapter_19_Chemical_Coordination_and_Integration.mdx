# Chemical Coordination and Integration

## Key Concepts

### Endocrine System and Hormones
Hormones are non-nutrient chemicals that act as intercellular messengers, produced in trace amounts by ductless (endocrine) glands. They provide coordination and integration for cellular functions that are not directly innervated by the neural system.

### Major Endocrine Glands and Hypothalamus
1.  **Hypothalamus:** The basal part of the forebrain; produces releasing (e.g., GnRH) and inhibiting (e.g., somatostatin) hormones that regulate the pituitary.
2.  **Pituitary Gland:**
    *   **Anterior Pituitary:** Secretes GH (growth), PRL (milk), TSH (thyroid), ACTH (adrenal), LH, FSH (gonads), and MSH (melanocytes).
    *   **Posterior Pituitary:** Stores and releases oxytocin and vasopressin (ADH).
3.  **Pineal Gland:** Secretes **melatonin**, which regulates the 24-hour diurnal rhythm (sleep-wake cycle).
4.  **Thyroid Gland:** Produces T3, T4 (regulate BMR and metabolism) and thyrocalcitonin (TCT, regulates blood calcium).
5.  **Parathyroid Glands:** Secrete **PTH**, which increases blood Ca2+ levels (hypercalcemic).
6.  **Thymus:** Secretes **thymosins**, which play a major role in the immune system (T-lymphocyte differentiation).
7.  **Adrenal Glands:**
    *   **Medulla:** Secretes emergency hormones (Adrenaline, Noradrenaline).
    *   **Cortex:** Secretes corticoids like cortisol (carbohydrate metabolism) and aldosterone (electrolyte/water balance).
8.  **Pancreas (Composite Gland):**
    *   **Alpha cells:** Secretes **glucagon** (increases blood sugar).
    *   **Beta cells:** Secretes **insulin** (decreases blood sugar).
9.  **Gonads:**
    *   **Testis:** Produces **androgens** (e.g., testosterone) for male sexual development and spermatogenesis.
    *   **Ovary:** Produces **estrogen** (female characteristics) and **progesterone** (supports pregnancy).

### Hormones from other organs
*   **Heart:** Atrial Natriuretic Factor (ANF) decreases blood pressure.
*   **Kidney:** Erythropoietin stimulates RBC production.
*   **G-I Tract:** Gastrin, secretin, CCK, and GIP regulate digestive juices.

### Mechanism of Hormone Action
Hormones bind to specific proteins called **hormone receptors**:
*   **Membrane-bound receptors:** Used by protein/peptide hormones; they generate **second messengers** (e.g., cAMP) to regulate metabolism.
*   **Intracellular receptors:** Used by steroid hormones and iodothyronines; they interact with the genome to regulate gene expression and chromosome function.
