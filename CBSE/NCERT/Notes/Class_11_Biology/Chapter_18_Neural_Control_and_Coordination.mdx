# Neural Control and Coordination

## Key Concepts

### Neural System and Coordination
Coordination is the process where two or more organs interact and complement each other. The neural system provides an organized network for quick point-to-point connections, while the endocrine system provides chemical integration through hormones.

### Human Neural System
The human neural system is divided into two parts:
1.  **Central Neural System (CNS):** Comprises the brain and the spinal cord; it is the main site for information processing and control.
2.  **Peripheral Neural System (PNS):** Includes all the nerves associated with the CNS.
    *   **Afferent Fibres:** Transmit impulses from tissues/organs to the CNS.
    *   **Efferent Fibres:** Transmit regulatory impulses from the CNS to tissues.
    *   **Somatic Neural System:** Relays impulses from the CNS to skeletal muscles.
    *   **Autonomic Neural System:** Transmits impulses from the CNS to involuntary organs and smooth muscles (further divided into Sympathetic and Parasympathetic).

### Neuron: Structural and Functional Unit
A neuron consists of three main parts:
*   **Cell Body:** Contains cytoplasm, organelles, and Nissl’s granules.
*   **Dendrites:** Short fibers that transmit impulses towards the cell body.
*   **Axon:** A long fiber that transmits nerve impulses away from the cell body to a synapse.
    *   **Myelinated Axons:** Enveloped with myelin sheath; gaps between sheaths are called nodes of Ranvier.
    *   **Unmyelinated Axons:** Lack a myelin sheath.

### Generation and Conduction of Nerve Impulse
1.  **Resting State:** The axonal membrane is polarized (positive outside, negative inside) due to a higher concentration of K+ inside and Na+ outside, maintained by the Na+/K+ pump.
2.  **Depolarisation:** Stimulation makes the membrane permeable to Na+, causing an influx that reverses the polarity and generates an action potential.
3.  **Repolarisation:** Permeability to K+ increases, restoring the resting potential.

### Synaptic Transmission
Transmission occurs at a **synapse** (pre-synaptic and post-synaptic neurons separated by a synaptic cleft).
*   **Electrical Synapse:** Rare in humans; impulse flows directly between neurons.
*   **Chemical Synapse:** Involves neurotransmitters (like Acetylcholine). Arrival of an impulse at the axon terminal releases these chemicals from synaptic vesicles into the cleft to bind with receptors on the next neuron.

### Central Neural System: The Human Brain
The brain is protected by the skull and three cranial meninges (dura mater, arachnoid, and pia mater).
1.  **Forebrain:**
    *   **Cerebrum:** Divided into left and right hemispheres (connected by corpus callosum). Cerebral cortex (grey matter) is for higher brain functions.
    *   **Thalamus:** Coordinating centre for sensory/motor signaling.
    *   **Hypothalamus:** Regulates body temperature, hunger, thirst, and hormone secretion.
2.  **Midbrain:** Contains corpora quadrigemina and the cerebral aqueduct.
3.  **Hindbrain:**
    *   **Pons:** Interconnects different regions of the brain.
    *   **Cerebellum:** Coordinates motor activity and balance.
    *   **Medulla:** Controls respiration, cardiovascular reflexes, and gastric secretions.
