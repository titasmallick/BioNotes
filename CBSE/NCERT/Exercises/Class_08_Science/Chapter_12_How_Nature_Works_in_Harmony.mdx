# Exercises - Chapter 12: How Nature Works in Harmony

## Questions and Answers

### 1. Refer to the given diagram (Population ? Community ? Ecosystem) and select the wrong statement.
(i) A community is larger than a population.
(ii) A community is smaller than an ecosystem.
(iii) An ecosystem is part of a community.

**Answer:**
**Statement (iii) is wrong.** An ecosystem is not a part of a community; rather, a community (along with the abiotic environment and their interactions) is a part of an **ecosystem**. The correct hierarchy is: Individual $\rightarrow$ Population $\rightarrow$ Community $\rightarrow$ Ecosystem.

---

### 2. If all decomposers suddenly disappear from a forest ecosystem, what changes do you think would occur? Explain why decomposers are essential.

**Answer:**
If decomposers (like bacteria and fungi) disappear:
*   **Waste Accumulation:** Dead plants, fallen leaves, and animal remains would pile up indefinitely as they would not be broken down.
*   **Nutrient Depletion:** Nutrients trapped in dead matter would not be returned to the soil.
*   **Plant Death:** Without recycled nutrients, the soil would lose its fertility, and plants would eventually stop growing and die.
*   **Ecosystem Collapse:** Since plants (producers) are the base of all food chains, their death would lead to the death of all consumers.
**Conclusion:** Decomposers are essential because they **recycle nutrients** back into the environment, ensuring the continuity of life.

### 3. Selvam shared that his village was less affected by the 2004 Tsunami due to the presence of mangrove forests. How did mangroves protect the village?

**Answer:**
Mangrove forests act as a **natural buffer or shield**. Their dense root systems and thick vegetation:
1.  **Slow down waves:** They absorb the energy of strong tsunami waves and storm surges, reducing their height and speed before they reach human settlements.
2.  **Windbreaks:** They slow down strong winds during storms.
3.  **Soil Stabilization:** Their roots hold the soil firmly, preventing coastal erosion during floods.

### 4. Look at this food chain: Grass $\rightarrow$ Grasshopper $\rightarrow$ Frog $\rightarrow$ Snake. If frogs disappear, what will happen to the population of grasshoppers and snakes? Why?

**Answer:**
*   **Grasshoppers:** Their population will **increase** rapidly because their natural predator (the frog) is gone. This may lead to over-grazing of grass.
*   **Snakes:** Their population will **decrease** because their primary source of food (the frog) is no longer available. Some snakes might die of starvation or be forced to find other prey.

### 5. In a school garden, students noticed fewer butterflies. What could be the reasons? What steps can they take?

**Answer:**
*   **Possible Reasons:** Use of synthetic pesticides in the garden, loss of host plants (where they lay eggs), or lack of flowering plants (nectar source).
*   **Steps to take:**
    1.  **Plant a Pollinator Garden:** Grow a variety of brightly coloured, nectar-rich flowering plants.
    2.  **Avoid Pesticides:** Stop using chemicals that harm beneficial insects.
    3.  **Provide Host Plants:** Identify and plant specific plants that local butterflies use for laying eggs (e.g., curry leaf plants, citrus).

---

### 6. Why is it not possible to have an ecosystem with only producers and no consumers or decomposers?

**Answer:**
An ecosystem needs **balance and recycling**. 
*   Without **consumers**, the producer population would grow unchecked until they compete for space and resources, potentially leading to a crash.
*   Without **decomposers**, nutrients would stay locked in dead plants. Once the soil nutrients are exhausted, the producers would not be able to grow.
**Conclusion:** All three roles are necessary to maintain a **dynamic and sustainable cycle** of energy and matter.

---

### 7. Human-made ecosystems like agricultural fields are necessary, but they must be made sustainable. Comment.

**Answer:**
Human-made ecosystems meet our needs for food and resources but often use **monoculture** (growing one crop) and **synthetic chemicals** (fertilisers/pesticides). These practices can lead to:
*   **Soil Degradation:** Loss of fertility and beneficial soil microbes.
*   **Pollution:** Chemicals leaching into groundwater.
*   **Loss of Biodiversity:** Habitats for birds and insects are destroyed.
To be **sustainable**, these ecosystems should adopt **organic farming**, crop rotation, and natural pest control to protect the environment for future generations.

---

### 8. If the Indian hare population drops because of a disease, how would it affect the number of other organisms in a food web?

**Answer:**
*   **Predators (Fox, Eagle):** Their numbers would likely **decrease** due to the scarcity of prey (the hare).
*   **Producers (Grass and Plants):** Their numbers might **increase** initially because there are fewer hares to eat them.
*   **Other Herbivores (e.g., Deer):** They might face **increased competition** for space if plants grow excessively, or they might face **increased predation pressure** if predators like eagles and foxes switch their focus to them.
