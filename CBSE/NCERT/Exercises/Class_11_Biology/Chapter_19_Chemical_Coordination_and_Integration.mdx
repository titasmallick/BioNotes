# Chemical Coordination and Integration - Exercises

**1. Define the following:**
(a) **Exocrine gland:** Glands that have ducts and release their secretions into them.
(b) **Endocrine gland:** Ductless glands whose secretions (hormones) are released directly into the blood.
(c) **Hormone:** Non-nutrient chemicals that act as intercellular messengers and are produced in trace amounts.

**2. Diagrammatically indicate the location of the various endocrine glands in our body.**
*(Note: Locations described for textual format)*
- **Brain:** Hypothalamus, Pituitary, Pineal.
- **Neck:** Thyroid and Parathyroid.
- **Chest:** Thymus.
- **Abdomen:** Pancreas, Adrenal (above kidneys), Ovaries (in females).
- **Pelvic region:** Testis (in scrotal sacs in males).

**3. List the hormones secreted by the following:**
- (a) **Hypothalamus:** GnRH, Somatostatin, etc.
- (b) **Pituitary:** GH, PRL, TSH, ACTH, LH, FSH, MSH (Anterior); Oxytocin, Vasopressin (Stored in Posterior).
- (c) **Thyroid:** Thyroxine (T4), Triiodothyronine (T3), Thyrocalcitonin (TCT).
- (d) **Parathyroid:** Parathyroid hormone (PTH).
- (e) **Adrenal:** Adrenaline, Noradrenaline (Medulla); Cortisol, Aldosterone, Androgenic steroids (Cortex).
- (f) **Pancreas:** Insulin (beta cells), Glucagon (alpha cells).
- (g) **Testis:** Androgens (Testosterone).
- (h) **Ovary:** Estrogen, Progesterone.
- (i) **Thymus:** Thymosins.
- (j) **Atrium:** Atrial Natriuretic Factor (ANF).
- (k) **Kidney:** Erythropoietin.
- (l) **G-I Tract:** Gastrin, Secretin, CCK, GIP.

**4. Fill in the blanks:**
- (a) Hypothalamic hormones: **Pituitary**
- (b) Thyrotrophin (TSH): **Thyroid**
- (c) Corticotrophin (ACTH): **Adrenal cortex**
- (d) Gonadotrophins (LH, FSH): **Gonads (Testis/Ovary)**
- (e) Melanotrophin (MSH): **Melanocytes**

**5. Write short notes on the functions of the following hormones:**
- (a) **PTH:** Increases blood Ca2+ levels by bone resorption and reabsorption from kidneys and food.
- (b) **Thyroid hormones:** Regulate basal metabolic rate (BMR), carbohydrate/protein/fat metabolism, and RBC formation.
- (c) **Thymosins:** Major role in T-lymphocyte differentiation and antibody production.
- (d) **Androgens:** Regulate male sex organ development and spermatogenesis.
- (e) **Estrogens:** Stimulate female secondary sex character development and follicle growth.
- (f) **Insulin and Glucagon:** Jointly regulate blood glucose levels (Insulin lowers, Glucagon raises).

**6. Give example(s) of:**
- (a) Hyperglycemic hormone: **Glucagon**; Hypoglycemic hormone: **Insulin**
- (b) Hypercalcemic hormone: **PTH**
- (c) Gonadotrophic hormones: **LH, FSH**
- (d) Progestational hormone: **Progesterone**
- (e) Blood pressure lowering hormone: **ANF**
- (f) Androgens and estrogens: **Testosterone** and **Estradiol**

**7. Which hormonal deficiency is responsible for the following:**
- (a) Diabetes mellitus: **Insulin**
- (b) Goitre: **Iodine (Thyroid hormones)**
- (c) Cretinism: **Thyroid hormones (during pregnancy)**

**8. Briefly mention the mechanism of action of FSH.**
**Answer:** FSH binds to membrane-bound receptors on target ovarian cells. This interaction generates second messengers (e.g., cAMP), which trigger biochemical changes and physiological responses (e.g., follicle growth).

**9. Match the following:**
- (a) T4 - (ii) Thyroid
- (b) PTH - (iv) Parathyroid
- (c) GnRH - (i) Hypothalamus
- (d) LH - (iii) Pituitary
